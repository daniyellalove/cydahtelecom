How We Work

A clear process from concept to closeout.

We integrate with your existing project team — architects, engineers, IT, and ownership — to keep low-voltage design aligned with the overall project from the first meeting to the final punchlist.

We work alongside your project team.

Cydah coordinates directly with the full design and ownership team at each phase — not in isolation.

Architects MEP Engineers IT Departments Ownership Groups Project Managers Contractors Vendors & Carriers AHJ
01
Discovery & Planning

Understand your environment, goals, and constraints.

Review program requirements, owner standards, and existing conditions to understand how technology supports operations, care models, and growth. This phase sets the foundation for all design decisions — ensuring low-voltage systems are aligned with how the facility will be operated, staffed, and experienced by residents or patients.

Program Review

Owner standards, technology roadmaps, and operational requirements that shape design decisions.

Existing Conditions

Review of existing systems, infrastructure, and site constraints for additions or renovations.

Team Kickoff

Coordination with architects, MEP engineers, and IT to establish workflows and design integration points.

02
Design (SD, DD, CD)

Develop coordinated drawings ready for bidding and construction.

Develop and refine technology drawings and documentation that integrate with architectural and MEP sets, providing coordinated information for bidding and construction. Each phase adds detail and precision — from schematic layouts to construction-ready documents with specifications, schedules, and contractor notes.

Schematic Design (SD)

High-level system layouts, room layouts, and technology concepts aligned with the architectural program.

Design Development (DD)

Detailed device layouts, pathway routing, MDF/IDF planning, and coordination with other MEP disciplines.

Construction Documents (CD)

Full construction-ready drawings with notes, schedules, specifications, and contractor instructions.

03
Construction Support

Stay engaged from groundbreaking to final punchlist.

Remain engaged through construction administration to help interpret the design, respond to field questions, and verify low-voltage systems are installed as intended. Consistent CA presence ensures design intent is maintained and that field conditions or substitutions are properly evaluated before they affect the finished product.

Submittals & Shop Drawings

Review of contractor-submitted product data, shop drawings, and substitutions against the design intent.

RFIs & Field Questions

Timely responses to requests for information from contractors and field teams during construction.

Site Walks & Closeout

Site observation visits, punchlists, and review of as-built and closeout documentation.

Let's walk through your project together.

Whether you're at SD or already into DD, we can integrate quickly and add clear value to your team's existing process.
